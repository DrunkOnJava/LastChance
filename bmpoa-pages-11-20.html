Chapter V: Wildlife Safety

Blue Mountain's location provides habitat for diverse wildlife. Understanding how to coexist safely with our wild neighbors is essential for all residents.

üêª BEAR SAFETY CRITICAL üêª

Bears are active year-round. Following these guidelines protects you, your family, and the bears.

Black Bear Safety

Prevention is Key

  • Remove bird feeders April-November (required by law)
  • Secure garbage in bear-resistant containers
  • Clean grills after each use
  • Don't leave pet food outside
  • Keep car doors and windows closed
  • Install motion-sensor lights

If You See a Bear

‚úÖ DO:

  • Remain calm and back away slowly
  • Make yourself appear large
  • Make noise - talk firmly
  • Give the bear space to escape
  • Fight back if attacked

‚ùå DON'T:

  • Run (bears can run 35 mph)
  • Make direct eye contact
  • Turn your back on the bear
  • Play dead
  • Feed or approach bears
‚öñÔ∏è Virginia Law: Feeding bears is illegal and punishable by fines up to $2,000. Residents who attract bears through negligence may be held liable for damages.
Blue Mountain Property Owners Association ‚Ä¢ Page 11

Other Wildlife

Deer

White-tailed deer are abundant and can cause vehicle accidents and garden damage.

  • Drive slowly, especially at dawn and dusk
  • Use deer-resistant plants in gardens
  • Install 8-foot fencing for complete protection

Wild Turkey

Wild turkeys are common and generally harmless but can be territorial during breeding season.

  • Don't feed turkeys
  • Make noise if they block roadways
  • Keep distance during spring (March-May)

Coyotes

Coyotes are occasionally seen and generally avoid humans.

  • Keep pets indoors at night
  • Don't leave small pets unattended
  • Remove attractants (pet food, garbage)

Venomous Snakes

Timber rattlesnakes and copperheads inhabit the area.

  • Watch where you step and place hands
  • Wear boots when hiking
  • Keep grass cut short around homes
  • If bitten, call 911 immediately

Chapter VII: Construction Guidelines

All construction and exterior modifications in Blue Mountain require Architectural Review Committee (ARC) approval. These guidelines ensure our community maintains its character and property values.

ARC Review Process

1

Submit Application

Complete ARC application with detailed plans, materials list, and site survey. Applications available at BMPOA.org.

2

Review Period

ARC has 30 days to review and respond. Complex projects may require additional time or committee meeting presentation.

3

Approval & Permits

Upon ARC approval, obtain necessary county permits. Warren County Building Department: (540) 635-2435.

4

Construction

Follow approved plans exactly. Changes require new ARC approval. Maintain proper construction hours and site cleanliness.

5

Final Inspection

Schedule ARC final walkthrough within 30 days of completion. Address any deficiencies promptly.

Required Approvals

Always Required:

  • New construction
  • Additions to homes
  • Outbuildings & sheds
  • Decks & patios
  • Driveways & walkways
  • Fencing
  • Exterior color changes

May Be Exempt:

  • Interior renovations
  • Roof repairs (same material/color)
  • Window replacement (same style)
  • Landscaping (non-structural)
  • Temporary structures (<30 days)
