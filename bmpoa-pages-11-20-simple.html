Chapter V: Wildlife Safety

Blue Mountain's 2,100-acre community supports diverse wildlife populations including black bears, white-tailed deer, wild turkeys, and over 120 bird species. Shenandoah Valley's expanding bear population requires comprehensive safety protocols for human-wildlife coexistence.

Black Bear Safety Protocols

Prevention Measures (Risk Reduction 90%+)

  • Remove bird feeders April 1-November 30 (Virginia Code §29.1-521, fines up to $500)
  • Bear-resistant containers: Certified IGBC (Interagency Grizzly Bear Committee) standards
  • Grill maintenance: Clean immediately after use, store propane indoors
  • Pet food storage: Sealed containers inside garages/homes only
  • Vehicle security: Windows up, doors locked, no food visible
  • Lighting systems: Motion-activated LED lights, 1000+ lumens recommended
  • Electric fencing: 7,000+ volt systems for gardens/beehives

Bear Encounter Response Protocol

Defensive Actions (Distance >50 ft):

  • Stop movement immediately - sudden motion triggers chase response
  • Raise arms overhead - appear larger (6+ ft effective height)
  • Speak calmly and loudly - "Hey bear, going away now"
  • Back away slowly - maintain eye contact, no sudden movements
  • Create escape route - ensure bear has exit path
  • Bear spray ready - effective range 20-30 ft, 98% success rate

Dangerous Actions (Never Do):

  • Running - bears run 35+ mph, triggers chase instinct
  • Direct eye contact - perceived as threat/dominance challenge
  • Cornering bear - defensive attack likely when trapped
  • Playing dead - only for defensive attacks, not predatory
  • Feeding attempts - creates dangerous food conditioning
  • Flash photography - startles and agitates bears

Other Wildlife

White-tailed Deer

Abundant population causes vehicle accidents and property damage.

  • Drive slowly during dawn and dusk hours
  • Use deer-resistant plantings in gardens
  • Install 8-foot fencing for complete garden protection

Wild Turkey

Generally harmless but territorial during spring breeding season.

  • Avoid feeding turkeys
  • Make noise if blocking roadways
  • Maintain distance during March-May period

Venomous Snakes

Timber rattlesnakes and copperheads inhabit the area.

  • Watch placement of hands and feet when outdoors
  • Wear protective boots when hiking
  • Maintain short grass around residences
  • Seek immediate medical attention for bites (call 911)

Chapter VII: Construction Guidelines

All construction and exterior modifications require Architectural Review Committee (ARC) approval to maintain community character and property values.

ARC Review Process

1

Application Submission

Submit complete ARC application with detailed construction plans, materials specifications, and property survey. Applications available at BMPOA.org.

2

Committee Review

ARC reviews submissions within 30-day period. Complex projects may require additional review time or committee presentation.

3

Permit Acquisition

Following ARC approval, obtain required Warren County permits. Building Department: (540) 635-2435.

4

Construction Phase

Adhere strictly to approved plans. Plan modifications require new ARC approval. Maintain permitted construction hours and site management standards.

5

Final Inspection

Schedule ARC completion inspection within 30 days of project completion. Address identified deficiencies promptly.
