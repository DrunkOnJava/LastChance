Chapter V: Wildlife Safety

Blue Mountain's location provides habitat for diverse wildlife. Understanding safe coexistence with our wild neighbors is essential for all residents.

BEAR SAFETY CRITICAL

Bears are active year-round. Following these guidelines protects residents and wildlife.

Black Bear Safety

Prevention Measures

  • Remove bird feeders April-November (required by Virginia law)
  • Secure garbage in bear-resistant containers
  • Clean grills thoroughly after each use
  • Never leave pet food outside overnight
  • Keep vehicle doors and windows closed
  • Install motion-sensor lighting around homes

Bear Encounter Protocol

Recommended Actions:

  • Remain calm and back away slowly
  • Make yourself appear larger
  • Speak in a firm, calm voice
  • Provide escape route for bear
  • Fight back if physically attacked

Actions to Avoid:

  • Running (bears reach 35 mph)
  • Direct eye contact
  • Turning back on bear
  • Playing dead
  • Feeding or approaching bears
Virginia Law: Feeding bears is illegal, punishable by fines up to $2,000. Property owners may be held liable for bear-related damages caused by negligent attractants.
Blue Mountain Property Owners Association • Page 11

Other Wildlife

White-tailed Deer

Abundant population causes vehicle accidents and property damage.

  • Drive slowly during dawn and dusk hours
  • Use deer-resistant plantings in gardens
  • Install 8-foot fencing for complete garden protection

Wild Turkey

Generally harmless but territorial during spring breeding season.

  • Avoid feeding turkeys
  • Make noise if blocking roadways
  • Maintain distance during March-May period

Venomous Snakes

Timber rattlesnakes and copperheads inhabit the area.

  • Watch placement of hands and feet when outdoors
  • Wear protective boots when hiking
  • Maintain short grass around residences
  • Seek immediate medical attention for bites (call 911)

Chapter VII: Construction Guidelines

All construction and exterior modifications require Architectural Review Committee (ARC) approval to maintain community character and property values.

ARC Review Process

1

Application Submission

Submit complete ARC application with detailed construction plans, materials specifications, and property survey. Applications available at BMPOA.org.

2

Committee Review

ARC reviews submissions within 30-day period. Complex projects may require additional review time or committee presentation.

3

Permit Acquisition

Following ARC approval, obtain required Warren County permits. Building Department: (540) 635-2435.

4

Construction Phase

Adhere strictly to approved plans. Plan modifications require new ARC approval. Maintain permitted construction hours and site management standards.

5

Final Inspection

Schedule ARC completion inspection within 30 days of project completion. Address identified deficiencies promptly.

Chapter VIII: Nature & Conservation

Blue Mountain's natural environment represents our community's greatest asset. From spring wildflower displays to autumn foliage, our mountain ecosystem provides year-round natural beauty.

Thompson Wildlife Management Area

Spring Trillium Display

Thompson Wildlife Management Area, located 15 minutes from Blue Mountain, hosts one of Virginia's most spectacular natural displays. Each spring, approximately 18 million Great White Trilliums create an extraordinary carpet of white blooms across the forest floor.

Peak Bloom Period: Late April through Early May
Trail Access: 1.5-mile interpretive loop trail
Best Viewing: Mid-morning hours for optimal lighting
