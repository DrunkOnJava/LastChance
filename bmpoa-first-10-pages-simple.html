Committee Structure

Evacuation Procedures

Blue Mountain is divided into evacuation zones for emergency response. Familiarize yourself with your zone and primary/alternate routes.

Evacuation Zones

  • Zone 1 (Red): Highest priority - immediate evacuation
  • Zone 2 (Orange): High priority - 30 minutes
  • Zone 3 (Yellow): Standard evacuation - 45 minutes
  • Zone 4 (Green): Staged evacuation - 1 hour
  • Zone 5 (Blue): Shelter in place until directed

Emergency Kit Essentials

Documents & Communication

  • ID and insurance papers
  • Cash and credit cards
  • Phone chargers
  • Emergency contact list

Supplies

  • 3-day water supply
  • Non-perishable food
  • Medications
  • Flashlights & batteries

Chapter III: Fire Safety

Professional fire management practices protect our mountain community

CRITICAL FIRE SAFETY NOTICE

Open burning is STRICTLY PROHIBITED at all times within BMPOA boundaries. This policy is more restrictive than Warren County regulations and is enforced for community safety.

Violations constitute a Class 3 misdemeanor with fines up to $500

Fire Regulations & Compliance

PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

  • Open burning of any material
  • Burn barrels or burn piles
  • Campfires or fire pits
  • Outdoor fireplaces (open flame)
  • Burning of brush, leaves, or debris
  • Recreational fires

PERMITTED ACTIVITIES

  • Gas grills (with safety precautions)
  • Enclosed containment devices
  • Charcoal grills (designated areas)
  • Professional equipment only
  • Approved community events

FireWise Community Certification

Blue Mountain maintains certification as a FireWise community through the National Fire Protection Association, demonstrating our commitment to wildfire risk reduction through comprehensive education, planning, and community action.

Grilling & Cooking Safety Protocols

Required Safety Measures

  • Maintain minimum 10-foot clearance from all structures
  • Keep fire extinguisher or water source immediately accessible
  • Never leave cooking fires unattended
  • Ensure complete extinguishment before leaving area
  • Store propane tanks away from heat sources

Ash & Ember Disposal

  • Use metal containers with tight-fitting lids
  • Allow complete cooling (minimum 48 hours)
  • Store containers away from combustible materials
  • Dispose only when completely cold
  • Never use cardboard, plastic, or wooden containers

Defensible Space Management

Zone Distance Requirements Maintenance
Zone 1 0-30 feet Remove all dead vegetation, maintain irrigated plants Monthly inspection
Zone 2 30-100 feet Create fuel breaks, remove ladder fuels Quarterly assessment
Zone 3 100+ feet Thin forest density, remove dead trees Annual evaluation

The Lodge

The Blue Mountain Lodge serves as our community gathering place, available for member rentals and community events.

Lodge Features

  • Main Hall: Seats 120 for dining, 150 for presentations
  • Commercial Kitchen: Full catering capabilities
  • Stone Fireplace: Original 1960s construction
  • Covered Deck: Mountain views, seats 40
  • AV Equipment: Projector, screen, sound system
