Chapter I: Welcome & Governance

Welcome to Blue Mountain! This guide contains essential information for residents of our mountain community. Whether you're a new resident or a longtime member, this guide will help you navigate community life, safety procedures, and available services.

Board of Directors

The Blue Mountain Property Owners Association is governed by a seven-member volunteer board elected by the community.

Position Name Responsibilities
President Board Member Overall leadership, board meetings
Vice President Board Member Support president, special projects
Secretary Board Member Meeting minutes, correspondence
Treasurer Board Member Financial oversight
Directors (3) Board Members Committee leadership
Contact the Board: All board communications should be sent to the official BMPOA mailing address: P.O. Box 114, Linden, VA 22642

Committee Structure

BMPOA operations are supported by several committees that handle specific aspects of community management:

Active Committees

  • Architectural Review Committee (ARC): Reviews and approves construction projects
  • Social Committee: Organizes community events and gatherings
  • Lodge Access Committee: Manages lodge rentals and maintenance
  • Roads Committee: Oversees road maintenance and improvements
  • FireWise Committee: Focuses on fire safety and prevention

Board Meetings

Regular board meetings are held on the second Monday of each month at 6:00 PM at The Lodge (540 Cliff Road). All residents are welcome to attend.

Meeting Schedule:
  • Regular meetings: 2nd Monday, 6:00 PM
  • Annual meeting: First Saturday in May
  • Special meetings: As needed (announced via email)

Getting Involved

Volunteers are always needed for committees. Contact any board member to learn about opportunities to contribute to your community.

Evacuation Procedures

Blue Mountain is divided into evacuation zones for emergency response. Familiarize yourself with your zone and primary/alternate routes.

Evacuation Zones

  • Zone 1 (Red): Highest priority - immediate evacuation
  • Zone 2 (Orange): High priority - 30 minutes
  • Zone 3 (Yellow): Standard evacuation - 45 minutes
  • Zone 4 (Green): Staged evacuation - 1 hour
  • Zone 5 (Blue): Shelter in place until directed

Emergency Kit Essentials

Documents & Communication

  • ID and insurance papers
  • Cash and credit cards
  • Phone chargers
  • Emergency contact list

Supplies

  • 3-day water supply
  • Non-perishable food
  • Medications
  • Flashlights & batteries
Stay Informed: Monitor local radio (AM 1050, FM 95.3) and Warren County emergency alerts for evacuation orders and updates.

Chapter III: Fire Safety

🔥 CRITICAL FIRE SAFETY 🔥

Open burning is FORBIDDEN AT ALL TIMES within BMPOA boundaries. This is more restrictive than Warren County regulations.

Violations: Class 3 misdemeanor, fines up to $500

Fire Regulations

❌ Prohibited Activities

  • Open burning of any kind
  • Burn barrels or burn piles
  • Campfires or fire pits
  • Outdoor fireplaces (open flame)
  • Burning of brush, leaves, or debris

✅ Permitted Activities

  • Gas grills (with precautions)
  • Enclosed fire containment devices
  • Charcoal grills (in designated areas)

Grilling Safety

Required Precautions:
  • Keep 10+ feet from structures
  • Have water/extinguisher ready
  • Never leave unattended
  • Properly dispose of ashes (cool, metal container)

🚨 Report Fire Violations

Fire safety violations should be reported immediately:

  • Active fire: Call 911
  • Violations: Contact BMPOA Board
  • Warren County Fire Marshal: (540) 635-4357

FireWise Community

Blue Mountain is a certified FireWise community, committed to reducing wildfire risk through education and community action.

Defensible Space Zones

  • Zone 1 (0-30 feet): Remove all dead vegetation, maintain irrigated plants
  • Zone 2 (30-100 feet): Create fuel breaks, remove ladder fuels
  • Zone 3 (100+ feet): Thin forest, remove dead trees

Home Hardening

Roof & Gutters

  • Class A fire-rated materials
  • Clean gutters regularly
  • Screen roof vents

Vegetation

  • Plant fire-resistant species
  • Maintain irrigation
  • Remove dead branches

Recommended Fire-Resistant Plants

  • Trees: Maple, Cherry, Dogwood
  • Shrubs: Azalea, Mountain Laurel, Rhododendron
  • Ground Cover: Hostas, Ferns, Wild Ginger
FireWise Activities: Annual community projects include brush clearing, education workshops, and home assessments. Volunteer participation helps maintain our certification.

Chapter IV: Community Life

The Lodge

The Blue Mountain Lodge serves as our community gathering place, available for member rentals and community events.

Lodge Features

  • Main Hall: Seats 120 for dining, 150 for presentations
  • Commercial Kitchen: Full catering capabilities
  • Stone Fireplace: Original 1960s construction
  • Covered Deck: Mountain views, seats 40
  • AV Equipment: Projector, screen, sound system

Rental Information

Event Type Member Rate Deposit
Family Gatherings $100 $50
Community Events $150 $75
Private Parties $200 $100
How to Book: Email bluemountainlodgebooking@gmail.com with your event details, preferred dates, and contact information. Bookings require 2-week advance notice.

Lodge Guidelines

  • BMPOA members only (guests must be accompanied)
  • No smoking anywhere on premises
  • Music ends at 10:00 PM
  • Clean-up is renter's responsibility
  • Report damages immediately

Deer Lake Recreation

Deer Lake is available for BMPOA members with a valid recreation pass (issued annually in May).

Lake Rules:
  • Pass required for all visitors
  • Swimming at your own risk - no lifeguard
  • Fishing permitted (VA license required)
  • No motorized watercraft
  • Dogs must be leashed
  • Pack out all trash

Contact: bmpoadeerlake@gmail.com for recreation pass information.
