I. Welcome to Blue Mountain

Welcome to Blue Mountain, a unique residential community nestled in the beautiful mountains of Warren County, Virginia. Our community, established in 1968, spans approximately 3,000 acres and is home to over 450 property owners who value the natural beauty, privacy, and sense of community that Blue Mountain offers.

About This Guide

This comprehensive guide serves as your primary reference for understanding the governance, regulations, and services that help maintain Blue Mountain as a desirable place to live. Whether you're a new resident or a long-time property owner, this guide will help you:

  • Understand your rights and responsibilities as a property owner
  • Navigate community regulations and covenant requirements
  • Access important emergency preparedness information
  • Learn about available community services and amenities
  • Connect with community governance and committees

Our Community Vision

Blue Mountain strives to preserve its natural environment while providing a safe, well-maintained community for all residents. We balance individual property rights with community standards that protect property values and quality of life for all.

Important First Steps for New Residents

  1. Register your contact information with the BMPOA office
  2. Review the restrictive covenants that apply to your property
  3. Familiarize yourself with emergency evacuation routes
  4. Consider joining a committee to get involved in the community

Community History

Blue Mountain's story began in 1968 when the original developers envisioned a mountain community that would offer both privacy and community amenities. The Blue Mountain Property Owners Association (BMPOA) was established to manage common areas and ensure the community developed according to the original vision.

Key Milestones

Year Milestone
1968 Blue Mountain subdivision established
1975 Deer Lake constructed
1982 Sanitary District formed
1990 First community center built
2005 Major road improvement project completed

Community Growth

Over the decades, Blue Mountain has grown from a small mountain retreat to a thriving community of over 450 property owners. This growth has been carefully managed to preserve the natural beauty and character that makes Blue Mountain special.

Evacuation Routes

Know Your Routes

Blue Mountain has two primary evacuation routes:

  1. Blue Mountain Road - Primary route down the mountain
  2. Freezeland Road - Secondary route to Linden

Practice these routes with your family before an emergency occurs.

Fire Safety Zones

Creating defensible space around your home is critical for wildfire protection:

  • Zone 1 (0-30 feet): Remove all flammable materials
  • Zone 2 (30-100 feet): Reduce and space vegetation
  • Zone 3 (100+ feet): Thin trees and remove ladder fuels
